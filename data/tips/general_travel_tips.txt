# General Travel Tips

Always carry a photocopy of your passport separately from the original.

Learn basic phrases in the local language — hello, thank you, and excuse me go a long way.

Book flights on Tuesdays and Wednesdays for the best prices on average.

Travel insurance is worth it, especially for international trips. Medical emergencies abroad can cost thousands.

Use a VPN when connecting to public WiFi at airports and hotels.

Pack a universal power adapter if visiting multiple countries.

Arrive at international airports at least 3 hours before departure.

Street food is often the most authentic and affordable way to experience local cuisine. Look for stalls with high turnover — fresh food and lots of locals are good signs.
